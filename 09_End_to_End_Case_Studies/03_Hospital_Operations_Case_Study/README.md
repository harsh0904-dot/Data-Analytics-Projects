# Hospital Operations Case Study

## Project Overview

- Domain: Healthcare Analytics
- Focus: Patient flow, treatment outcomes, hospital cost, and operational efficiency
- Tools: SQL, Python, Power BI

## Business Problem

- Hospital leadership needs a clearer view of how patient volume, treatment patterns, and cost are affecting operations.
- The current reporting approach does not make it easy to connect clinical performance with financial impact.

## Business Objective

- Measure hospital activity through patient volume, outcome distribution, and treatment cost.
- Identify the conditions and treatments that create the highest operational burden.
- Support leadership decisions around cost control, service efficiency, and patient care quality.

## Key Business Questions

- Which conditions contribute the highest total treatment cost?
- Which states or regions contribute the largest patient load?
- How do treatment outcomes compare across medical conditions?
- Which treatments are associated with higher cost or longer stays?
- Where can hospital leadership improve efficiency without reducing quality of care?

## Suggested Analytical Approach

- Clean and standardize the patient operations dataset.
- Analyze patient volume, cost, outcomes, satisfaction, and operational patterns.
- Use SQL and Python to validate KPIs and explore high-cost segments.
- Build a Power BI dashboard for executive, clinical, and financial monitoring.

## Expected Deliverables

- Clean hospital patient dataset
- SQL queries for KPI validation and condition-level analysis
- Python scripts for cleaning, EDA, and feature engineering
- Multi-page healthcare operations dashboard
- Operational insight summary with recommendations

## Expected Insights

- High-cost and high-volume medical conditions
- Outcome distribution across clinical categories
- Treatment patterns associated with longer length of stay
- Opportunities to improve hospital efficiency and cost visibility

## Business Recommendations

- Monitor high-cost conditions more closely through regular KPI tracking.
- Improve resource planning for high-volume and long-stay patient groups.
- Review treatment pathways that create heavy operational burden.
- Align financial and clinical reporting to support better leadership decisions.

## Skills Demonstrated

- Healthcare KPI analysis
- Patient and treatment segmentation
- SQL-based operational analysis
- Python-based data preparation and clinical trend exploration
- Power BI dashboard design for multi-stakeholder reporting

## Why This Case Study Is Portfolio-Ready

- Healthcare analytics is a high-impact domain with strong storytelling value.
- The project combines operational, clinical, and financial analysis in one business case.
- It is highly presentable because the business value is clear and the reporting flow is intuitive.
